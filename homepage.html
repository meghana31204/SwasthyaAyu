FAQ's

What is Ayurvedic medicine?
Ayurvedic medicine is an ancient system of natural healing that originated in India over 5,000 years ago. It focuses on balancing the body, mind, and spirit through herbal treatments, diet, and lifestyle changes.
Is Ayurvedic treatment safe for all ages?
Ayurvedic treatments are generally safe for all ages when used correctly. However, it is important to seek advice from a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner, especially for children, elderly individuals, and those with pre-existing medical conditions.
Are Ayurvedic medicines free from side effects?
Ayurvedic medicines are generally considered safe, especially when taken under the guidance of a qualified practitioner. However, incorrect usage or allergic reactions to certain herbs may cause side effects, so it's important to follow recommendations carefully.
Do I need a prescription for Ayurvedic medicines?
Some Ayurvedic medicines may require a prescription from a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner, especially for personalized treatments. Over-the-counter Ayurvedic products may be available, but it is recommended to seek professional advice for specific concerns.
What are the common herbs used in Ayurvedic medicine?
Common herbs used in Ayurveda include Ashwagandha (for stress and energy), Turmeric (for inflammation and healing), Triphala (for digestion), Brahmi (for mental clarity), and Tulsi (for immune support).
